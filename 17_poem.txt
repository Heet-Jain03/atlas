POEM: Morning Coffee
=====================

Before the world demands its due,
before the calendar bleeds red,
there is this:
a ceramic warmth between two palms,
steam curling upward like a question
not yet asked.

The first sip is always the truest —
bitter, then soft,
the way most honest things arrive.

Outside, the street is waking slowly,
pigeons negotiating the gutter,
a bus sighing to a stop.

But here, in this cup,
there is enough world
for now.
