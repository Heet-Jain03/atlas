SHORT STORY: The Last Train
============================
The platform was empty except for an old man and a rusted bench.

Marcus checked his watch: 11:58 PM. Two minutes until the last train.
He hadn't taken this route in twenty years, not since leaving Carrow
Creek as a young man full of ambition and empty of patience.

The town had called him back with a single telegram: "Father is gone."

A distant rumble grew into a roar. Headlights cut through the fog.
The train slowed, sighing to a stop, its doors sliding open like an
invitation.

Marcus picked up his single bag and stepped aboard.

Through the window, as the train pulled away, he saw the old water
tower — faded blue letters still reading CARROW CREEK: POP. 312.

He hadn't expected to feel anything. He was wrong.
