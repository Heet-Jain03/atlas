BOOK REVIEW: "The Midnight Library" by Matt Haig
==================================================
Rating: 4.5 / 5 Stars

This novel is a thought-provoking exploration of regret, choice,
and the meaning of life. The story follows Nora Seed, who finds
herself in a magical library between life and death, where each
book represents a different life she could have lived.

WHAT I LOVED:
- The concept is original and beautifully executed
- Characters feel genuine and relatable
- Emotional depth without being preachy

WHAT COULD BE BETTER:
- Some middle sections feel slightly repetitive
- The ending wraps up a bit too neatly

OVERALL: Highly recommended for anyone going through a difficult
period or simply questioning their choices in life. A warm, hopeful,
and ultimately uplifting read.
